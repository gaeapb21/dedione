## Who Is DediOne Actually For?

Honestly, DediOne's pricing structure tells you exactly who they're targeting:

**The $12.99/yr VPS crowd** — developers with side projects, students experimenting with Linux, people who need a persistent node for a bot, a VPN endpoint, or a lightweight web app. At that price, it costs less than a single lunch to run a server for a year.

**The storage hoarder** — the HDD VPS line, with Raid10 arrays going up to 8 TB for $40/month, is clearly aimed at people who need bulk cheap storage with some redundancy. Backup servers, media libraries, self-hosted Nextcloud — that kind of thing.

**The Asia-Pacific business** — the Hong Kong dedicated server lineup with China Premium Network optimization is the standout for anyone whose users are primarily in mainland China. BGP routing through HK isn't magic, but it's a common and practical approach.

**The budget dedicated server buyer** — $30/month for a Xeon E3 with 16–32 GB RAM, 500 GB SSD plus 8 TB HDD, and unlimited bandwidth is a legitimate deal for a small business that's outgrown shared hosting.

Across DediOne's product lineup, a few things are consistent:
